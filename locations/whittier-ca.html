Earthquake history

Whittier Narrows 1987 and its lasting effect on plumbing

The 1987 Whittier Narrows earthquake, magnitude 5.9, had its epicenter near Whittier and caused significant damage across the eastern LA County area. Ground movement of that scale can open supply line joints that were previously tight, and a connection stressed in 1987 that did not immediately fail may have been slowly deteriorating for the decades since. Older homes in Whittier, particularly those on the original 1920s-40s pipe systems, are worth inspecting when a supply leak develops, since the joint failure may trace back to seismic stress rather than simple age. The 2014 La Habra earthquake at magnitude 5.1 added another stress event to the same pipe systems.

Old Town and historic districts

Pre-war plumbing and galvanized pipe

Old Town Whittier along Greenleaf Avenue and the surrounding historic residential streets are among the few areas in the eastern LA County region with pre-1940 housing still in active residential use. Galvanized steel supply lines from that era have typically corroded internally to a fraction of their original bore after eight or nine decades of use. A home in this part of Whittier with low water pressure across all fixtures, rust-colored water at the tap when it first runs, or a pinhole leak in an exposed supply run is likely at the end of the galvanized pipe's service life. We assess the full supply line condition and give an honest view of whether a spot repair or a whole-house repipe makes more sense for the specific pipe.
