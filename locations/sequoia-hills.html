Root intrusion

What mature trees do to buried drain lines

A sewer or drain line with a cracked joint becomes an attractive target for roots seeking moisture. Once roots find the joint, they expand it and eventually fill the pipe interior, slowing drainage first and then causing backups. A camera inspection of the drain line from the cleanout confirms root intrusion and shows how far into the pipe it has progressed. In Sequoia Hills, where mature plantings are the rule rather than the exception, a drain that is slowing or backing up deserves a camera look before a chemical treatment is assumed to be sufficient.

How do you remove roots from a drain line?

After camera confirmation, mechanical cutting with a rotating blade clears the roots, and a follow-up camera confirms the line is clear. We also discuss whether the joint that let the roots in needs a trenchless liner repair to prevent recurrence.
