The streets around Valley View Avenue and Rosecrans Avenue form the commercial and civic core of La Mirada, and the residential blocks just behind them are among the oldest in the city. Most were built during the original Olive Mirada Ranch development that started in 1953 and ran through the early 1960s. That puts the copper supply lines in these homes at sixty-plus years of service under Suburban Water Systems water, which runs hard at 9 to 12 grains per gallon from its Metropolitan Water District source. At that age and under that water chemistry, pinhole leaks in the supply lines are not unusual.

The housing stock

Original copper and galvanized in 1950s-60s homes

Homes on the residential streets near the La Mirada Theatre and along the blocks toward Excelsior Drive were built on slab-on-grade foundations with copper or galvanized steel supply lines run through and under the concrete. Galvanized steel corrodes from the inside and narrows over decades until the flow drops noticeably and the pipe wall fails. Copper of this era pinholes from the hard imported water working on the pipe interior over sixty-plus years. Both failure modes are common in this part of La Mirada, and both show up as either a slab leak under the floor or a failure at a supply connection inside the wall.

What is the most common leak type in Downtown La Mirada homes?

Pinhole leaks in original copper supply lines run under or through the slab. The copper in homes from the 1950s and 60s has been under hard MWD-sourced water for sixty-plus years, and pinholes are a predictable result.

Do you work on commercial properties in the Downtown corridor?

Yes. Retail, restaurant, and office properties along Valley View and Rosecrans are part of our service area. Commercial leaks come with written detection and repair documentation.

How do you find a slab leak without tearing up the floor?

Acoustic correlators and thermal imaging locate the leak through the concrete before anything is cut, so the repair opening targets only the confirmed fault point.

Is the water in Downtown La Mirada hard?

Yes. Suburban Water Systems delivers water sourced from the Metropolitan Water District, which draws from the Colorado River and State Water Project. It runs at 9 to 12 grains per gallon, which is hard and accelerates pipe wear over time.
