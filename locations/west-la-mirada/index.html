Irrigation prevalence

Larger yards and multi-zone systems

West La Mirada properties often have somewhat larger side yards than the more compact tracts closer to the city center, and those yards frequently carry multi-zone drip and spray irrigation systems. A zone that has been running continuously or a lateral that has split below the surface is a common West La Mirada call in the warmer months, when the water bill is the first thing that flags a zone that did not shut off. We test each zone at the controller, check the solenoid valves in the valve box, and walk the lateral line to find the failure before assuming anything needs excavating.

Slab supply

1960s-70s copper and the MWD factor

Like the rest of La Mirada, West La Mirada homes from the 1960s and 70s have copper supply lines that have carried Suburban Water Systems water sourced from the Metropolitan Water District at 9 to 12 grains per gallon hardness for fifty to sixty years. A slab-line pinhole in this part of the city is the same detection and repair job as anywhere else in La Mirada: acoustic correlators on the floor, thermal imaging to confirm the zone, a targeted core, and a repair sized to the confirmed fault point. Homes remodeled in the late 1970s or 1980s may also have polybutylene branch lines mixed into the original copper system.
