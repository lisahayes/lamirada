Residential blocks

1960s-70s homes off the arterial

The residential streets running north and south off Foster Road are largely 1960s-70s single-family homes, on slab-on-grade pads with copper supply lines that have now carried Suburban Water Systems water at 9 to 12 grains per gallon hardness for fifty to sixty years. Homes in this era that were remodeled or extended in the late 1970s or 1980s may also have polybutylene branch lines mixed in with the original copper mains. A water bill that has climbed without explanation, or a warm spot on a tile floor, is the typical first sign of a slab-line pinhole in the supply run.
