The service line

From the meter to the house

Service lines in La Mirada's 1960s-70s development were typically copper or galvanized steel, buried at a depth appropriate for the mild Mediterranean climate where freeze protection was not a factor. After fifty to sixty years, galvanized steel has often corroded significantly along its run, and copper has had decades of hard MWD water, at 9 to 12 grains per gallon, passing through it. Either material can develop a leak anywhere along the buried length, and the wet patch in the yard rarely sits directly over the fault. Electronic signal tracing and acoustic correlators are the tools that locate the fault without opening the full run.

Questions we hear

Answered for La Mirada homeowners

Why does a longer service line mean more risk?

A longer buried line has more length exposed to soil movement, corrosion, and root pressure. It also creates a larger zone to search when a meter test confirms a loss, which is why we use electronic signal tracing to map the pipe path before any excavation.

How do I confirm a leak is in the service line and not inside the house?

An overnight meter test with all interior fixtures and irrigation off confirms a pressurized loss somewhere between the meter and the house. That tells us the leak is in the service line, yard plumbing, or the main shutoff, rather than an interior supply run.

What is an electronic signal trace?

A tone generator is connected to the metal pipe at a fitting, and a receiver wand above the surface maps the pipe path and identifies where the signal drops, marking the fault location. It also confirms the pipe routing when the original installation was not documented.

Are Buena Vista homes on a slab?

Most 1960s-70s La Mirada construction used slab-on-grade foundations, and Buena Vista is no exception. Supply lines pass under or through the concrete, which is why slab leaks are a consideration for interior supply faults.

Do you handle both yard and interior leaks on the same call?

Yes. If the meter test suggests a yard loss and there are also interior signs, we can address both in the same visit and provide separate detection and repair quotes for each.
