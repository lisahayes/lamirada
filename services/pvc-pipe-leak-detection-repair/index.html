Plastic pipe behaves nothing like copper. PVC and its hot-water cousin CPVC do not corrode, so their leaks come from cracks and from joints that were solvent welded. A joint glued in a hurry, a run stressed by movement, sunlight that left an exposed length brittle, or a rare cold La Mirada night can all open a split. How plastic fails

Joints, cracks, and sunlight

The solvent-welded joint is the usual suspect. When the cement was applied unevenly or the pipe was not held while it cured, the bond never fully formed, and it weeps under pressure. Cracks come from impact, from a run flexed by settling, or from cold on the rare night it reaches the pipe. Sunlight is the quiet one: PVC left exposed for years grows brittle from UV and splits where it was once flexible. Each cause sets a slightly different repair.

Where PVC is used

Irrigation, drains, and cold supply

PVC carries cold water, irrigation, and drain, waste, and vent lines, while CPVC handles hot supply in some homes. That spread means a PVC leak can show up as a wet flower bed over an irrigation line, a damp cabinet under a drain, or a seep behind a wall on a cold run. Detection

Pressure first, then eyes on the run

For a pressurized PVC or CPVC line, a pressure test shows whether the run holds, which confirms a leak before we chase it. Exposed and accessible plastic often shows the split or the weeping joint on a close look. For hidden runs, acoustic listening picks up the escape, and on irrigation we isolate zones to find the failing one. The aim is the same as always: pinpoint before opening anything.

The plastic specifics

Schedule, primer, and a proper bell

Good PVC work lives in the details. Schedule 40 pipe needs the right primer to soften the surface before cement, and the fitting socket, the bell, has to be held seated while it sets or it backs out and weeps. On drain, waste, and vent lines a dry fit slip can leave a hairline gap at a hub. Out in the yard an irrigation manifold packs many glued joints into a small space, so one rushed weld there is a common source. We treat the joint as the job, because on plastic the joint is where the leak lives.

Repair

Cut, weld, and cure it right

A PVC repair is clean when it is done patiently. We cut out the cracked or failed section, fit a new piece, and solvent weld it with the cure time the joint actually needs rather than rushing it back into service. Where sunlight caused the failure, shielding or burying the exposed run keeps the next one from going the same way. A common misconception

Insurance will cover the plumbing itself

Standard homeowners coverage typically pays for the drywall, flooring, and cabinets damaged in a pvc pipe event. It does not usually pay to replace the copper pipe that failed. That distinction lives in the fine print.

Questions we hear

Answered for La Mirada homeowners

What usually causes a PVC pipe to leak?

A solvent-welded joint that did not bond fully, a crack from movement or impact, or brittleness from years of sun on an exposed run. PVC does not corrode, so these are the common causes.

What is the difference between PVC and CPVC?

Both are plastic, but CPVC tolerates hot water and is used on some hot supply lines, while PVC handles cold water, irrigation, and drain lines.

Can a cracked PVC pipe be patched?

The lasting fix is to cut out the failed section and solvent weld in a new piece with proper cure time. A rushed patch tends to weep again.

Why did my exposed PVC become brittle?

Ultraviolet light. PVC left in the sun for years grows brittle and splits. Shielding or burying the run prevents a repeat.

Where is PVC most likely to leak in my yard?

Irrigation lines are a frequent source. A wet patch or an oddly lush spot over a buried zone often points to a cracked PVC irrigation line.
