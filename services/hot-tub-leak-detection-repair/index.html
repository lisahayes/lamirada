A portable hot tub is a self-contained unit, and almost all of its plumbing lives inside a cabinet wrapped in foam insulation. When it leaks, the water absorbs into that foam and soaks the cabinet floor before it ever shows outside. You often notice it as a hot tub that empties faster than it should, a heater that struggles to hold temperature, or a cabinet that smells of mildew. The foam insulation, which is the hot tub's best thermal asset, is the thing that hides the leak longest.

Inside the cabinet

Union fittings, the pump, and the heater

The equipment bay on one side of the cabinet holds the circulation pump, the heater, and the control system. Everything connects through union fittings, the threaded couplings with an o-ring inside that allow the components to be removed for service. These o-rings are the most common leak source: they harden under constant heat, the union backs off slightly under vibration, and water weeps from the joint. A pump seal that has worn, or a heater manifold connection that has loosened, is the next suspect. Opening the equipment panel and running the tub reveals which fitting is the one.

The jet manifold

Where the plumbing branches to every jet

From the pump the water feeds a PVC manifold that branches to each jet, running through the foam insulation to the jet bodies embedded in the acrylic shell. A manifold fitting that has vibrated loose or a small crack in a PVC branch drips into the foam rather than the cabinet floor, making it one of the harder hot tub leaks to trace without running the unit while the cabinet is open. We do exactly that, watching the manifold connections while the pump runs at each setting, since some leaks only show under pressure and not at low flow.

The shell

Acrylic cracks and fitting holes

The acrylic or fiberglass shell of a hot tub can crack under point loads or from an impact, and cracks at the jet holes are a stress point because the shell is thinnest there after the opening is cut. A hairline crack in the acrylic usually shows as a fine white line in the surface color. Jet bodies seal to the shell with a rubber gasket and a locking nut on the inside, and that seal is a leak point when the nut backs off. We fill the tub and inspect the shell interior with the jets off, then run the jets and recheck, because some shell leaks seal themselves under hydrostatic pressure and only open when the pump creates a flow path.

Detection and cost

The wet-test method

Finding a hot tub leak is a wet-test: we fill the tub, remove the cabinet panels, and run it through each setting while watching every union, the pump seal, the heater connections, and the manifold. Whatever wets under load names the source. A union o-ring swap or a pump seal is an inexpensive, contained fix. A manifold repair is somewhat more involved. A shell crack in the acrylic is typically repaired with a two-part acrylic filler. We tell you the scope and the cost before anything is opened further. A common misconception

The leak has to be visible to be real

When hot tub comes up in a La Mirada home, the leak is often already there but hidden. A pressurized loss inside a slab or wall cavity produces no visible water for weeks. The bill and the meter tell you it is real before your eyes do.

Questions we hear

Answered for La Mirada homeowners

Why does my hot tub lose water even when not in use?

A union fitting or plumbing connection inside the cabinet is weeping even when the pump is off. Gravity still drains water from a low fitting. The foam insulation absorbs it before it shows outside.

What are union fittings?

Threaded couplings in the hot tub plumbing with an o-ring inside that seal the connection. They allow components to be removed for service. The o-rings harden under heat and are the most common hot tub leak source.

Why does my hot tub smell of mildew?

Water from a cabinet leak soaks the foam insulation, which stays warm and wet, a perfect condition for mildew. The smell often comes before the water shows outside the cabinet.

Can a cracked acrylic shell be repaired?

Yes, most hairline cracks in an acrylic shell are repaired with a two-part acrylic filler. A large structural crack is a different scope. We assess the crack before recommending a repair.

How do you find a leak inside the foam insulation?

We open the cabinet panels, fill the tub, and run it at each setting while watching every connection. Leaks that only appear under pump pressure are found this way.
